Hi there, I'm Sicheng He!

I am a 4th year PhD student from MDO lab in University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. I am fortunate enough to be advised by Prof. Martins. I enjoy doing research in the computational science -- especially solving PDE and optimization problem. I have a strong interests for both the mathematical formulation and its implementation (check our research philosophy in MDO lab). I have been doing research on flutter constrained aircraft MDAO (my thesis work); mixed-integer convex programming formulations for structural optimization. Recently, I spend some time on applying machine learning to our airfoil data set, take a look at my labmates' excellent work!

I have used many mathematical tools. Three of them, in my opinion, are most elegant and useful: Newton-Krylov method (especially the Jacobian-free version), adjoint method (especially the coupled, multiphyics version), automatic differentiation (it has been used for ADjoint and neural network).

Newton-Krylov method... $$ \begin{aligned} & \frac{\partial \mathcal{R}}{\partial q}\Delta q=-\mathcal{R}^{i},\\ & q^{i+1} = q^{i} + \alpha\Delta q. \end{aligned} $$

Adjoint method... $$ \begin{aligned} & \left(\frac{\partial \mathcal{R}}{\partial u}\right)^\top\psi=\frac{\partial f}{\partial u},\\ & \frac{df}{dx} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} - \psi^\top \frac{\partial \mathcal{R}}{\partial x}. \end{aligned} $$

Automatic differentiation... $$ \begin{aligned} & \text{forward AD:} \frac{\partial \mathcal{R}}{\partial q}\Delta q,\\ & \text{backward AD:} \left(\frac{\partial \mathcal{R}}{\partial u}\right)^\top\psi. \end{aligned} $$
