Hi there, I'm Sicheng He!

I am a 4th year PhD student from MDO lab in University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. I am lucky enough to be advised by Prof. Martins. I enjoy doing research in the computational science -- especially solving PDE and optimization problem. I have been doing research on flutter constrained aircraft MDAO; mixed-integer convex programming formulations for structural optimization. Recently I spend some time on applying machine learning to our airfoil data set, take a look at my labmates' excellent work!

Among the mathematical tools I used, I think: Newton-Krylov method is the KING; adjoint method is the QUEEN; automatic differentiation is the JACK!

The King... $$ \begin{aligned} & \frac{\partial \mathcal{R}}{\partial q}\Delta q=-\mathcal{R}^{i},\\ & q^{i+1} = q^{i} + \Delta q. \end{aligned} $$

The Queen... $$ \begin{aligned} & \left(\frac{\partial \mathcal{R}}{\partial u}\right)^\top\psi=\frac{\partial f}{\partial u},\\ & \frac{df}{dx} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} - \psi^\top \frac{\partial \mathcal{R}}{\partial x}. \end{aligned} $$

The Jack... $$ \begin{aligned} & \text{forward AD:} \frac{\partial \mathcal{R}}{\partial q}\Delta q,\\ & \text{backward AD:} \left(\frac{\partial \mathcal{R}}{\partial u}\right)^\top\psi. \end{aligned} $$
